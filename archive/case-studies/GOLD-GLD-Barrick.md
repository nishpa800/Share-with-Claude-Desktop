# Case Study: Gold / GLD / Barrick (B) — Signal-Triggered Macro Trade

**Trigger:** Saw GLD (gold ETF) fire HV+D+PBJ (High Volume + Displacement + PBJ)
— possibly a 4th signal too — from the HVD-PBJ-PPD indicator study.

## The Logic

- Gold was getting murdered at the time
- Anish had a feeling it was coming to a settling point
- Believed a VOB line was going to form soon
- Put a stake in the ground: "I believe that signal is potentially going to be it"
- Bought GLD + complementary plays like Barrick Gold (B) around $35-37

## The Outcome / Lesson

- Had 1,000-2,000 shares, got a good pop
- Then realized the limitation of lines: "you don't know — it could keep going up,
  but it could also look for bounces and pop on the 4th bounce, and it depends on
  the timeframe you're on"
- On the daily timeframe, price usually doesn't just touch a line and go forever —
  "they got to manipulate, they got to fuck with your mind, make you feel pain"
  (the potential-energy / shakeout concept)

## Why This Matters For The System

1. **Detection plots work across asset classes** — this was an ETF, not a stock.
   ETFs let us run macro/commodity/diversification plays.
2. **Timeframe determines the bounce pattern** — reinforces the per-timeframe
   agent requirement.
3. **The line tells you WHERE but not the bounce count** — we need to model how
   many tests a line takes before the real move, per timeframe.
